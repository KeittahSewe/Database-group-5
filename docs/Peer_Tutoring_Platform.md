# PART A: PROJECT OVERVIEW

## PART A-1. Project Idea Definition 

**Project Topic**

The proposed system is a Peer Tutoring Platform , a web-based database driven application that connects students who need academic help, with qualified peer tutors within their institution.

**Problem Statement** 

Most students experience difficulties in their studies but often lack access to timely academic assistance . At the same time other students tend to process string knowledge in those subjects and can provide peer support. However, the absence of a structured platform to connect tutors and learners results in inefficiencies such as difficulty in identifying qualified tutors ,scheduling conflicts and lack of accountability .

Therefore, this Peer Tutoring Platform aims to address these challenges by providing a centralized system where students can register as tutors or learners , list subjects they can teach , request tutoring sessions ,schedule meetings and provide feedback after sessions. The system will store and manage information related to users, tutoring subjects, session bookings schedules and feedback.

By organizing this information within a well-structured relational database, the system will improve access to academic support, streamline tutoring coordination ,and enhance the overall learning experience.

The Peer Tutoring Platform is not merely a practical exercise; it is a practical tool for advancing educational equity. By structuring peer learning through a robust database system , the project operationalizes SDG 4’s vision :that quality education should be accessible ,inclusive, data-informed, and oriented toward developing well-rounded, capable individuals.

## PART A-2. Scope and Objectives

### **Scope**

The scope of the Peer Tutoring Platform focuses on managing the core data required to support peer-to-peer tutoring interactions within an academic environment

The system will cover the following functional areas:

* Student registration and profile management  
* Tutor availability and subject listings  
* Booking and scheduling tutoring sessions  
* Managing tutoring sessions records  
* Recording feedback and ratings after tutoring sessions  
* Maintaining subject and tutor information

NB:

The system will NOT be responsible for: financial payments, video conferencing infrastructure, or integration with external learning management systems platforms.

**Objectives**

The objectives of this Peer Tutoring Platform include:

* To design a relational database that efficiently stores and manages information related to students, tutors, subjects and tutoring sessions.  
* To facilitate the matching of students seeking academic assistance with suitable peer tutors based on subject expertise.  
* To support scheduling and management of tutoring sessions between students.  
* To demonstrate proper database design techniques including entity identification, normalization and relational schema development.

## PART A-3. Stakeholders

Several stakeholders are involved in the Peer Tutoring Platform system, each interacting with the system in different ways.

| Stakeholder | Role | System Interaction |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Student (Learners) | Primary users seeking tutoring help | Registers, searches tutors, books sessions,  provides feedback (submits ratings). |
| Peer Tutor | Student who offers tutoring services in subjects where they possess strong academic knowledge | Registers as tutor, list the subjects they can teach, sets availability, confirms/completes sessions |
| Administrator | Overseas (manage & maintain) the platform | Manages users, monitors sessions, maintains subject lists, ensures the integrity and security of the database, generates reports |
| Institution / Department | Indirect stakeholder | Reviews aggregate performance data and session reports |
